Section 01 — The Problem

Why one-angle analysis fails in geopolitics

Most news analysis tools give you one voice. One summary. One angle.

Geopolitical reality doesn't work that way. The same event looks completely different from Washington, Moscow, Brussels, or Beijing. Understanding the full picture means holding multiple informed perspectives at once — and that's cognitively expensive for a human analyst to do at speed.

PokoSpectrum automates that process.

Section 02 — What It Is

Three pillars, one intelligence pipeline

01

Multi-Agent Architecture

A system of specialized AI agents, each operating from a distinct geopolitical perspective. They don't agree. They're not supposed to.

02

RAG-Powered Knowledge

Agents are grounded in retrieved, current information — not just model memory. The system ingests live news, embeds it, and surfaces the right context to the right agent at query time.

03

Structured Article Output

The end product isn't raw data or bullet points. It's publication-ready analytical writing — structured, opinionated, and sourced.

Section 03 — How It Thinks

01 — Ingest

The pipeline monitors world news sources continuously, processes incoming articles, and indexes them into a vector store.

02 — Retrieve

When an event is flagged for analysis, each agent retrieves the context most relevant to its perspective from the shared knowledge base.

03 — Analyze

Independent agents reason over the retrieved context through their own geopolitical lens — producing divergent takes on the same event.

04 — Synthesize

Outputs are compiled into a structured multi-perspective article, ready for review or publication.

Section 04 — Stack & Approach

Python · RAG Architecture · Vector Embeddings · Agentic Orchestration · FastAPI · PostgreSQL · LLM APIs · News Ingestion Pipeline

Built entirely in Python. Designed for extensibility — new agents, new perspectives, new sources can be added without restructuring the core.
